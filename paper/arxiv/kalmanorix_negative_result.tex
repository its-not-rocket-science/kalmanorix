\begin{abstract}
We report a focused negative-result preprint for mixed-domain retrieval fusion. In the canonical domain-balanced C100 confirmatory run ($n_{\text{pairs}}=1193$), Kalman-minus-Mean $\Delta$ nDCG@10 is $-9.258801070226193\times10^{-6}$ with Holm-adjusted $p=1.0$; recall@100 delta is 0.0. Compute overhead is bounded (latency ratio 1.0722551296204925; FLOPs ratio 1.0). The benchmark verdict is \texttt{inconclusive\_sufficiently\_powered}. This preprint emphasizes reproducibility artifacts, claim scope, and cautious interpretation rather than broad methodology claims.
\end{abstract}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!8,colframe=black,title=Scope of conclusion]
This paper makes a \textbf{single bounded claim}: under the tested protocol (domain-balanced mixed-domain benchmark, \texttt{max\_candidates=100}, deterministic embedding regime), Kalman-style uncertainty fusion did \emph{not} demonstrate a reliable practical improvement over mean fusion.

It does \textbf{not} claim that Kalman methods are universally ineffective, nor that mean fusion is always best.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Setup (confirmatory track only)}
Benchmark: \texttt{mixed\_beir\_v1.2.0} with train=5078, validation=634, test=1193, minimum per-domain test count 126. Confirmatory settings: \texttt{domain-balanced=true}, \texttt{max\_candidates=100}, paired query-level analysis, Holm correction, and practical-effect gate.

Primary endpoint: paired $\Delta$ nDCG@10 (Kalman minus Mean). Practical threshold: $|\Delta|\ge 0.02$. Statistical threshold: Holm-adjusted $p<0.05$. Efficiency checks: latency ratio $\le 1.5$, FLOPs ratio $\le 1.1$.

\section{Main negative result}
The final confirmatory outputs (from \texttt{results/evidence\_registry.json} and \texttt{results/claim\_gate.json}) are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\Delta$ nDCG@10 $=-9.258801070226193\times10^{-6}$
  \item Holm-adjusted $p=1.0$
  \item $\Delta$ recall@100 $=0.0$
  \item Latency ratio (Kalman/Mean) $=1.0722551296204925$
  \item FLOPs ratio (Kalman/Mean) $=1.0$
  \item Verdict: \texttt{inconclusive\_sufficiently\_powered}
\end{itemize}

Interpretation: the run had enough power for the predeclared practical effect scale, but no practically meaningful Kalman-over-Mean gain was detected.

\section{What this result does and does not mean}
\textbf{What it supports.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item In this protocol, Kalman fusion did not clear the practical-significance and multiplicity-adjusted evidence gate.
  \item Mean fusion remains a strong low-complexity reference under the tested configuration.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{What it does not support.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item It does not establish universal inferiority of uncertainty-aware fusion.
  \item It does not rule out gains under different embeddings, domains, candidate budgets, or uncertainty estimators.
\end{itemize}
