\title{Powered, Uncertainty-Aware Evaluation Finds No Reliable Retrieval Gain from Kalman-Style Fusion in Mixed-Domain Search}

\begin{abstract}
We present a negative-result evaluation of uncertainty-weighted retrieval fusion under a powered mixed-domain protocol. Our thesis is that, under appropriately powered paired testing and multiplicity control, Kalman-style uncertainty fusion does not reliably outperform a mean-fusion baseline. On a domain-balanced C100 run with $n_{\text{pairs}}=1193$, Kalman-minus-mean nDCG@10 is $-9.258801070226193\times10^{-6}$ (Holm-adjusted $p=1.0$), recall@100 delta is $0.0$, latency ratio is $1.0722551296204925$, and FLOPs ratio is $1.0$. Thus, the accuracy difference is practically zero while latency is higher for Kalman-style fusion.

The main contribution is an evidence standard for retrieval claims: powered paired evaluation, explicit uncertainty reporting, and claim-gating that prevents overinterpretation of null effects. Compute-aware orchestration is retained as a secondary systems observation (useful for deployment trade-offs), not the paper's central claim.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Small retrieval deltas are easy to overclaim when power is low or uncertainty is underreported. We focus on a single evaluation question: does Kalman-style uncertainty-weighted fusion reliably beat mean fusion on mixed-domain retrieval when tested under a powered, paired protocol?

Our answer is no for the evaluated configuration. On a domain-balanced C100 run with 1,193 paired queries, the primary endpoint (nDCG@10) is essentially unchanged, with a tiny negative delta and adjusted $p=1.0$. We therefore present this work as a negative result and as a template for uncertainty-aware claim discipline in retrieval evaluation.

\section{Contributions}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Negative empirical finding (primary).} In powered mixed-domain evaluation, Kalman-style fusion does not reliably outperform mean fusion on nDCG@10.
  \item \textbf{Uncertainty-aware evaluation protocol.} We report paired deltas, adjusted significance, and efficiency ratios jointly, with explicit claim-gating.
  \item \textbf{Honest evidence taxonomy.} We explicitly mark the confirmatory slice as empty in this run and treat that as a limitation, not confirmatory support.
  \item \textbf{Secondary systems observation.} Compute-aware orchestration remains useful for practical operating-point control, but is not evidence of fusion superiority.
\end{itemize}

\section{Setup and Hypothesis}
\paragraph{Primary hypothesis.}
\textbf{H1.} Kalman-style fusion improves nDCG@10 over mean fusion on the domain-balanced C100 paired test set.

\paragraph{Evaluation protocol.}
We use paired query-level comparison with nDCG@10 as the primary metric \citep{jarvelin2002}, Wilcoxon signed-rank testing \citep{wilcoxon1945}, and Holm correction for multiplicity \citep{holm1979}. The benchmark follows mixed-domain retrieval practice in the spirit of heterogeneous IR evaluation \citep{thakur2021beir,voorhees2016}.

\paragraph{Claim gate.}
A positive claim requires: claim-ready benchmark status, non-empty confirmatory slice, positive primary effect, and adjusted significance. In this run, the gate is blocked.

\section{Discussion}
This paper's central conclusion is narrow and evidence-based: in this mixed-domain C100 evaluation, uncertainty-weighted Kalman-style fusion does not outperform mean fusion. The result should not be generalized to all domains or model families, but it directly falsifies a commonly expected gain in the tested setting.

Compute-aware orchestration remains relevant as a systems consideration for budgeted deployment, but that is a secondary engineering observation here. It should not be conflated with evidence that the fusion rule improves retrieval quality.

\section{Limitations}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Single configured run.} Results are specific to the domain-balanced C100 configuration and current implementation.
  \item \textbf{Empty confirmatory slice.} Confirmatory slice size is 0 in this run; therefore we do not treat this as confirmatory evidence and avoid confirmatory claim language.
  \item \textbf{External validity.} Additional datasets and replications are required before making broader statements about uncertainty-weighted fusion.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
Powered, uncertainty-aware evaluation on 1,193 paired mixed-domain queries shows no reliable advantage of Kalman-style fusion over mean fusion. The measured quality deltas are effectively zero (or slightly negative), adjusted significance is null, and latency is higher at matched FLOPs. We therefore frame this as a negative retrieval result and advocate stricter claim discipline for small reported gains.
